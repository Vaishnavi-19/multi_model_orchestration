### Who is Lord Krishna in Hinduism?

**Lord Krishna** is one of the most revered and worshipped deities in Hinduism, considered the **eighth avatar (incarnation) of Lord Vishnu**, the preserver god within the Trimurti (Hindu trinity). He is a central figure in the epic Mahabharata, particularly in the Bhagavad Gita, where he imparts profound spiritual wisdom to Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra.

Key aspects and roles of Lord Krishna:

*   **Divine Avatar:** He is believed to have descended to Earth to restore dharma (righteousness) and destroy evil.
*   **Playful Child and Youth:** His early life in Vrindavan is filled with stories of his mischievous childhood (as a butter thief, "Makhan Chor"), his divine play (leela) with the Gopis, and his enchanting flute playing that captivated all.
*   **Philosopher and Teacher:** As Arjuna's charioteer in the Mahabharata, he delivers the Bhagavad Gita, a discourse on duty, righteousness, selflessness, devotion, and the nature of reality.
*   **Warrior and King:** He played a pivotal role in the Kurukshetra war and later established the kingdom of Dwarka.
*   **Supreme Being:** For many Vaishnavites (devotees of Vishnu), especially those in the Gaudiya Vaishnava tradition (like ISKCON), Krishna is not just an avatar but the Svayam Bhagavan, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, from whom all other deities and avatars originate.
*   **Symbolism:** Krishna embodies divine love, joy, wisdom, courage, and the balance between worldly duties and spiritual liberation. He is often depicted with dark skin, wearing yellow robes, holding a flute, and adorned with peacock feathers.

---

### Who is Shiva in Hinduism?

**Shiva** is one of the principal deities of Hinduism, often referred to as "The Destroyer" within the Trimurti (the Hindu trinity of Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva, representing creation, preservation, and destruction/transformation, respectively). However, his role as a destroyer is not merely negative; it signifies the destruction of ignorance, evil, and the old order to pave the way for new creation and renewal.

Key aspects and roles of Shiva:

*   **The Destroyer and Transformer:** He is the force that brings about dissolution and rebirth, essential for the cosmic cycle.
*   **The Ascetic (Adi Yogi):** Shiva is revered as the Adi Yogi (the first yogi) and Adi Guru (the first guru), the source of yoga and meditation. He is often depicted meditating on Mount Kailash, covered in ash, with a third eye symbolizing wisdom and insight.
*   **Family Man:** Despite his ascetic nature, Shiva is also depicted as a householder, married to Parvati (Shakti) and father to Ganesha and Kartikeya.
*   **Lord of Dance (Nataraja):** His cosmic dance (Tandava) represents the rhythm of the universe, encompassing creation, preservation, destruction, illusion, and liberation.
*   **Benevolent and Fierce:** Shiva manifests in many forms, from the gentle and compassionate Bholenath (the innocent one) to the terrifying Bhairava.
*   **Symbolism:** He is often worshipped in the aniconic form of the Lingam. His iconography includes a crescent moon in his hair, the Ganga river flowing from his head, a snake around his neck, a trident (Trishula), and a drum (Damaru).
*   **Sect:** He is the primary deity in Shaivism, one of the major traditions of Hinduism.

---

### Who is Guru Raghavendra in Hinduism?

**Guru Raghavendra Swamy** (c. 1595 – 1671 CE) was a prominent **Hindu saint, philosopher, and theologian** who lived in the 17th century. He was a leading proponent and great exponent of the **Dvaita philosophy of Sri Madhvacharya**, which posits a fundamental distinction between God, the individual soul (Jiva), and matter (Prakriti).

Key aspects and significance of Guru Raghavendra Swamy:

*   **Dvaita Philosopher:** He was a highly influential pontiff (Peetadhipati) of the Madhva Matha (monastery), specifically the Sri Raghavendra Swamy Matha, and a staunch defender of Dvaita tenets through debates and extensive writings.
*   **Scholar and Teacher:** Born as Venkatanatha, he was known for his profound scholarship in the Vedas, Upanishads, and Dvaita philosophy. He imparted spiritual knowledge and guidance to countless disciples.
*   **Miracle Worker:** Many devotees believe he performed numerous miracles during his lifetime, healing the sick, helping the needy, and demonstrating his spiritual powers.
*   **Jiva Samadhi at Mantralayam:** In 1671, he entered *jiva samadhi* (a state of conscious self-entombment) at Mantralayam, a village in Andhra Pradesh, India. He declared that he would remain in the *Brindavan* (his sacred tomb shrine) for 700 years, providing blessings and guidance to his devotees.
*   **Continuous Presence:** Even today, Mantralayam is a major pilgrimage site, and millions of devotees believe that Guru Raghavendra Swamy continues to bless and intercede for them from his *Brindavan*. He is revered for his compassion, wisdom, and the power to grant wishes and alleviate suffering.
*   **Revered Saint:** He is a highly revered saint, particularly in the Madhva Vaishnava tradition, and is worshipped across South India and by followers globally.
